Paracharts audio logo

These audio logos were designed by Doug Firley of Shock City Studios. Here are some of his notes about their creation:

Below are the initial demo files for the Fizz Studios audio icon. These are melody and tone experiments to move to us toward our final audio icon product. From here we can further refine the icon's melody, speed it up or slow it down, play with the notes and its tone, and also play within the stereo field.

As far as the tone selections in these demos, I was aiming for something that would catch the attention and the listener would immediately understand it was introducing the audio description of a visual chart. I tried to design them so they wouldn't be too taxing on the the ears or brain after hearing it several times in a row.

Each section contains the audio icon and variations, standalone and included in speech context. The first section contains the audio files of Melody 1 demos, and the second contains Melody 2 demos.

Both melodies are based on the Fizz Studio logo. The logo has six bubbles, so each of the melodies has six notes. Both are in the key of F (for "Fizz").

I was trying to find tones that sat well within the computer generated frequencies of the sonification and ones that sounded cybernetic and not so far from the synthesized voice that it would seem like it didn't belong in the flow of the audio. You'll hear that some of the tones are one the brighter side, while others are more dull and muted. This is to help us find the right tone that is sounds appropriate, communicates what it is there for, and also has the least amount of hearing and mental fatigue.

There are two lengths we should also look at for the final result. One is the length of the sound of the melody and the second it the total length of the audio file. The length of the audio file is long enough to alert the listener of what it stands for. The second overall length, including any empty audio space at the beginning or ending we can adjust so that it paces out properly placed between the text audio and the graph audio. We want to make sure all three elements flow properly.

2025-3-2

Melody 1

Melody 1 basically plays each of the rows of bubbles of the logo individually with the pattern 1/1,2/1,2,3, with the notes rising in pitch.

  • In the key of F
  • The notes are F3/E3, G3/E3, F3, C4
  • The beats per minute is 180
  • The time signature is 3/4
Melody 2

Melody 2 was designed to be slightly more minimalist as a alternative option to Melody 1. Here the melody comprised of is two sets of three notes with the pattern 1,2,3/1,2,3, with the notes also rising in pitch.

  • In the key of F
  • The notes are F3,E3, G3/E3, F3, C4
  • The beats per minute is 180
  • The time signature is 4/4
